2. What the Research Says

Button Psychology — What Actually Works

🔵
Blue = Trust Reduces anxiety when personal info or payments are involved. Banks, healthcare, and safety-critical apps default to blue. It says "you're safe here."
🟡
Warm tones (coral, orange) = Action & warmth Coral combines the energy of red with the friendliness of orange. Less aggressive than pure red. Perfect for "join" or "get started" — it invites without pushing.
🟢
Green = Positive confirmation Best for success states, completion, and "yes" actions. Studies show 32% higher sign-up rates with green CTAs for positive affirmations.
🔴
Pure red = Urgency & danger Triggers fight-or-flight. Great for sales and time-limited offers. Not great for a community app where you want people to feel they belong. #FF5A5F sits right on this border.
Key insight: Airbnb started with #FF5A5F but they're a marketplace — they want urgency ("book now before it's gone!"). We're a community — we want belonging ("you're home here"). Our primary color should feel warm, not urgent.

The 60-30-10 Rule

The most effective visual identity formula, used by nearly every successful app:

60%
Neutral warmth — backgrounds, cards, space
30%
Typography & structure — text, icons, borders
10%
Brand accent — CTAs, highlights, key moments

Right now nomadspeople is closer to 60-15-25 — too much color competing for attention. Reducing accent surface area will immediately make the app feel calmer and more premium.

What Makes an App Feel Safe vs Circus-Like

🎪 Circus (what to avoid)🏡 Safe (what to aim for)
5+ accent colors across the interface 1-2 accent colors, each with a clear job
Every category gets its own color Categories use icons/emoji + neutral backgrounds
Multiple bold buttons per screen One primary CTA, everything else is secondary or text
Random font sizes across screens 3-4 predefined sizes on an 8px grid
Tight spacing, elements cramped 40%+ whitespace, generous padding
Different background tints per section One warm neutral background throughout
Colors chosen by "what looks nice here" Every color has a semantic meaning in the system

3. Proposed Color System

Based on the research, here's a direction that keeps nomadspeople's warmth while adding trust and calm. These are softer versions of what we already have — evolution, not revolution.

Primary — Warm Coral

coral #E8614D Primary CTA, key actions
coral-light #F08574 @ 25% Tint backgrounds, hover states

Shifted from pure red toward orange — warmer, friendlier, still energetic. Distinctly ours, not Airbnb's.

Success — Soft Sage Green

sage #34A853 Joined, confirmed, success
sage-light #34A853 @ 13% Success background tint

Softer, more natural green. Less neon, more "earth" — feels organic and trustworthy.

Accent — Warm Teal

teal #2A9D8F Secondary actions, links, info badges

Replaces the scattered blues. One cool accent that says "info, explore, secondary." Already close to our existing C.accent (#00A699) — we just need to actually use it.

Neutrals — Warm Foundation

bg #FAFAF9 Page background (keep)
surface #F5F3EF Cards, pills, inputs
border #E8E4E0 Dividers, separators

Our warm beige-grays are already excellent. Just need consistency — one tint everywhere, not six variations.

4. Button Hierarchy — The Rules

Every button in the app should fit one of these four levels. No exceptions.

Primary
Success
Secondary
Tertiary
LevelStyleWhen to UseRule
Primary Filled coral, white text, rounded Main action: join, save, continue, send Max 1 per screen
Success Filled sage green, white text After joining: "chat" button, confirmed states Only for completed positive actions
Secondary Outline teal, no fill Alternative actions: explore, filter, details Unlimited, but use sparingly
Tertiary Text only, gray or muted Low-priority: skip, cancel, maybe later No border, no background
The one-primary-per-screen rule is the single biggest change that will de-circus the app. When everything is bold, nothing is. Give each screen one clear focal point.

5. Category Colors — The Calm Solution

Currently: 12+ unique bright colors for activity categories. This is the biggest "circus" contributor. The fix:

ApproachHow It WorksFeel
Now Each category = unique bright color Coffee=#92400E, Food=#F97316, Night=#A855F7, Sport=#22C55E... Rainbow, chaotic, tiring
Option A Emoji + neutral background All categories use same warm gray background. The emoji IS the identity. Calm, clean, emoji do the talking
Option B 4 muted families only Group into: social (warm), active (teal), food (amber), culture (muted purple). Max 4 hues. Organized, not overwhelming

My recommendation: Option A for the map pins and category chips. We already have great emoji. Let them carry the personality — the UI should be the calm canvas behind them.

6. Typography — What We Have vs What Works

Our font weight system (FW) is solid: regular/medium/semi/bold/extra. But the sizes are freeform — each component picks its own s() value. A tighter scale creates rhythm:

RoleNow (scattered)Proposed (systematic)
Page titles(11)-s(14) variess(12) bold — always
Section headers(7)-s(8) variess(7.5) semi — always
Body / row texts(5.5)-s(7) variess(6.5) medium — always
Caption / metas(4)-s(5.5) variess(5) regular — always
Tiny labels(3.5)-s(5) variess(4) regular — always

Fewer sizes = more calm. The eye rests when it recognizes a pattern.

7. Spacing — The Invisible Differentiator

9. Implementation Roadmap

If you like this direction, here's the order I'd suggest:

PhaseWhatImpact
1. Foundation Update theme.ts with new color tokens: coral primary, sage success, teal accent. Add button hierarchy constants. Zero visual change yet — just the system.
2. Buttons first Create shared button components (PrimaryBtn, SuccessBtn, SecondaryBtn, TextBtn). Replace hardcoded buttons one screen at a time. Immediate "one voice" feeling.
3. Category colors Neutralize category colors to emoji-forward approach. Remove rainbow effect. Biggest visual calm improvement.
4. Background tints Consolidate 6+ background tints into 2: bg and surface. Consistency across screens.
5. Typography scale Standardize font sizes to the 5-level system. One pass per screen. Subtle but profound calm.
